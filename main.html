Deck OLED vs ROG Ally Z1 Extreme

Rog Ally's advantages compared to Deck OLED:

  • Z1E often delivers a 5-10% performance boost (can even be less powerful in certain scenarios)
  • Windows compatibility with editing softwares and Microsoft exclusives
  • 1080p, 120hz screen adjustable to 720p
  • Speakers have slightly more bass

Steamdeck OLED advantages compared to ROG Ally Z1E:

  • Runs Native SteamOS
  • Trackpads with haptic feedback
  • User friendly
  • More comfortable and ergonomic
  • More back buttons
  • Up to 2x the battery life
  • Much better for emulation
  • OLED, 90hz, 1000 nits brightness, 7.4 inch screen
  • SD Card slot that doesn’t overheat
  • Decky plugins for customization
  • Much more customizable
  • No annoying BSODs or constant updates

Disadvantages of ROG Ally Z1E compared to Deck OLED:

  • Windows instability (blue screens and crashes)
  • SD card slot issues
  • Frequent updates: Armoury Crate, MyASUS, BIOS, Drivers
  • AMD Adrenaline Software issues
  • SteamOS doesn’t work natively (yet)
  • Trigger squeaking after long usage
  • Short battery life
  • Hand fatigue after 30 mins of gaming

Deck OLED disadvantages compared to ROG Ally Z1E:

  • Compatibility limited for Windows-only applications
  • A bit less powerful
  • Lower screen resolution and refresh rate
  • Speakers have less bass
  • Static noise with wired headphones
  • Controller layout may take time to adjust to

Overall:
It really just depends on your choice.
If you mainly play Steam games → get the Deck OLED.
If you want Windows compatibility → go for the ROG Ally Z1E.
Love emulation? → Deck OLED all the way.
Play modern AAA games? → ROG Ally Z1E, but remember Deck OLED holds up too.
