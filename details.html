Find Which One Is Right For You!

Emulation?

Go for the SteamDeck OLED! The colors pop and the controls are extremely accurate. It can play some systems which Windows struggles with, such as PS2 and Xbox 360.

Performance (AAA Games)

The ROG Ally's powerful chip can run almost any AAA game at a comfortable 30 FPS, or modern AAA games at 25-30 FPS depending on graphic settings.

Have a Job?

The SteamDeck OLED's sleep feature is perfect for people living busy lives. It's truly pick-up-and-play. Plus, it doesn’t constantly demand annoying updates like the ROG Ally.

Long Sessions?

SteamDeck OLED all the way, with its 50Wh battery delivering 3–8 hours of battery life (content-dependent).

Functionality?

The SteamDeck OLED and ROG Ally tie here. The Ally shines for Windows-only apps and editing software. However, the Deck's trackpads become *way more important* for gamers and coders alike.

Major Problems…

The Deck OLED's major drawback is Linux compatibility issues for some apps. The ROG Ally's major drawbacks include poor battery life, SD card overheating, frequent driver updates, and discomfort during long sessions. The Deck may look bulkier, while some may prefer the Ally’s aggressive, RGB-heavy design.
